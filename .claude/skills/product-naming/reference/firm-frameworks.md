# Firm Frameworks (distilled)

The five generator subagents each carry their own framework. This file is the deeper reference + case studies, for when you want to explain or audit a school's logic.

## Lexicon Branding — sound symbolism + invent the experience
Pentium · BlackBerry · Swiffer · Sonos · Dasani · Febreze · Subaru Outback · Azure · Vercel.
- **Process:** Create → Invent → Implement. Strategy before words; generate ≥1,000, shortlist 50-100.
- **Strategy Diamond:** (1) What does winning look like? (2) What's at stake? (3) What asset must the name carry? (4) What single original idea must it say (an experience, not a description)?
- **Rules:** name the experience not the function; sound symbolism (V=energy, B/T=reliability, X/Z=fast, plosives=impact, light front vowels=light/slim, -ium=elemental strength); invented names cost less to build & signal innovation; **polarization is good** ("if the team is comfortable, it's not the right name").
- **Cases:** Pentium (-ium=strength, coined, premium) · BlackBerry (B=reliable, berry keys, "crazy"=polarizing win) · Swiffer (engineered sound of effortless swishing) · Sonos (palindrome = OS for sound both ways) · Dasani (light vowels = pure/light/refreshing).

## Catchword — brief-driven vocabulary, volume, storytelling, global
Asana · Upwork · VW Atlas · Starbucks Refreshers · Popcornmeter · Versant · Pippit.
- **6-step:** homework → creative brief → get creative (Project Vocabulary + Blue Sky, ~2,000 names) → shortlist ~10% → screen availability → research.
- **Rules:** "a name is a very short story"; three styles (descriptive/suggestive/empty vessel); favor CVCV, avoid clusters, design for global pronunciation; **code-name the project** to prevent attachment; **Law of Inverse Name Availability** (best names are taken); "research is a data point, not a decision point"; GPS multi-language screening.
- **Cases:** Asana (Sanskrit yoga pose, V-C-V global, balance) · Upwork ("up" your game) · Atlas (titan = size/strength; screened 19 languages) · Refreshers (suggestive, says the benefit) · Popcornmeter (popcorn=audience vs critic Tomatometer).

## Tanj (A Hundred Monkeys) — strategy-first, classify every name
Wii · Bravia · Ally · Nissan Juke · Coffee Mate Natural Bliss · FilmStruck.
- **6-step:** discovery → strategy → homework → generation → screening → presentation.
- **Three pillars (tag every candidate):** Construct (Real / Composite / Simplex[+Blend,+Shave] / Phrasal / Acronym) × Expression (Descriptive→Suggestive→Abstract) × Tone.
- **Rules:** map an existing word's meaning onto the product truth; anti-trend for longevity; systems thinking (family relates via tone/structure/meaning, not mechanical affixes); future-proof.
- **Cases:** Wii (we, lightly respelled, social play) · Bravia (coined premium) · Ally (someone on your side — bank rehab) · Juke (to outmaneuver — agile car) · Natural Bliss (truth + emotional payoff).

## Landor — positioning-first, 8 principles, elastic & systemic
FedEx · Enactus.
- **8 Principles:** 1 make it memorable · 2 fill it with meaning (expandable) · 3 say it out loud · 4 don't wait to fall in love · 5 listen to your fear (buzz of surprise) · 6 stand out in a crowd · 7 too much is never enough (first 100 are rejects) · 8 expect its story to evolve (elastic).
- **Rules:** start from positioning; capitalize on equity/how people already talk (FedEx = the colloquial shorthand); deliver a **nomenclature system**, not just a name (Fed + color-shifting Ex); carry meaning across the whole identity.
- **Cases:** FedEx (faster-sounding, drops "federal" baggage) · FedEx system (purple Fed anchor + per-division Ex) · Enactus (Entrepreneurial Action, transcends roots) · "The World On Time" · hidden-arrow wordmark.

## Igor — evocative-or-bust, provocation, white-space
Aria · truTV · Gogo · Boogie Board · WHOOP · Seven.
- **4 types:** Functional/Descriptive (argues AGAINST) · Experiential · Invented (poetic vs latinate) · **Evocative** (default — evokes positioning, like Uber/Virgin/Apple).
- **Taxonomy chart:** plot competitors on the 4 types × strength; generate toward the **empty quadrant**.
- **Provocation strategy:** take a word with strong/even negative connotation, slice it, map to positioning, redefine it (Virgin, Caterpillar, Yahoo, Gap).
- **Anti-"Happy Idiot":** reject morpheme-rationale coinages, foreign-word "passport" names, and Compound Wallflowers (Active/Arc/Atlas/Blue/Bridge/Edge/Flex/Scape/Span/Sync/Hub/Flow…). A name must work without explanation.
- **Association density (A.S.S. count):** favor words rich in cultural associations/idioms (Apple 15+, "Seven" = wonders/seas/sins/chakras).
- **Cases:** Aria (opera star turn — classy not themey) · WHOOP (visceral exclamation) · Boogie Board (board family + fun) · Gogo (reduplicative motion/ease, viral) · truTV (tru=real positioning).

## Cross-firm synthesis
Universal stages all five share: **strategy/brief before words → high-volume generation → shortlist ~5-10% → legal + cross-cultural screening → research as input not verdict.** Two recurring "trap" rules: *comfortable = wrong* (Lexicon) and *the best names are already taken* (Catchword).
